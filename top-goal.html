Top goal

Every day things come at you. Emails, chat notifications, requests from co-workers. All 'important', all vying for attention. If one allows their attention to be focused on the items coming in, they won't make progrss on the most important things.

The solution to this problem is to follow the practice of setting a 'Top Goal' for each day. At the end of each working day, I review my task list and calendar for the next day. From that list I choose a task that will have the greatest impact towards what I want to achieve. The next day, I spend the first 30 minuutes to an hour working on that task.

Following this will create space to work on the things that matter, rather than getting caught up in the day to day activities.

I found this principle in Matt Mochary's book The Great CEO Within.
